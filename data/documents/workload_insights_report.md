# Workload and Learning Correlation Report (Synthetic)

This report details how work activities (meetings, sprints, production support) impact learning timelines, providing recommended scheduling patterns.

## Work Signals Analysis
- **High Meeting Overhead (Ref Doc Sec 8):** Employees with more than **20 meeting hours per week** display a **60% drop** in learning plan completion rate. This is primarily due to fragmented attention windows.
- **Focus Windows (Ref Doc Sec 9):** Optimal learning progression occurs when employees have at least **15 focus hours per week** and less than **18 meeting hours**.
- **Engagement Strategy (Ref Doc Sec 10):** Reminders and learning slots should be scheduled during focus blocks. For employees with extreme workloads (>20 meeting hours), learning should be broken down into micro-learning segments (15-30 mins) rather than long blocks.
- **Communication Etiquette:** Do not push alerts during high-priority sync meetings or near known sprint release dates.
